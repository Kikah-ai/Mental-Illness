Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder

ADHD is one of the more common diagnosed neurodevelopmental disorders formed from childhood. While it is seen more commonly with children, this disorder usually follows them to adulthood. Children with ADHD may have trouble with controlling impulsive behaviours, focusing on a certain task, or be hyper and overly active.

- Spacing out
- Overly talkative
- Making continuous careless mistakes
- Inability to resist themselves, lack of self-control
- Difficulty of getting along with others

Predominantly Inattentive Presentation: The individual has a hard time organizing and completing a given task without being distracted. This is the same for following instructions and holding conversations.
Predominantly Hyperactive-Impulsive Presentation: A person with this has trouble sitting in one position for a long period of time. The person may fidget and be very talkative and impulsive. The Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) sponsors the National Resource Center on ADHD
The National Resource Center operates a call center with trained staff to answer questions about ADHD. The number is 1-800-233-4050.
